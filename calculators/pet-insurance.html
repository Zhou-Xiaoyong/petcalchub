Frequently Asked Questions

For most pet owners, yes — especially if you'd struggle to afford a surprise $3,000-$10,000 emergency vet bill. Pet insurance provides financial peace of mind and ensures you never have to choose between your pet's health and your finances. A single emergency surgery can cost $3,000-$5,000; cancer treatment can exceed $10,000. Pet insurance typically costs $30-$70/month for dogs and $15-$40/month for cats.

Most accident & illness policies cover: emergency vet visits, surgeries, hospitalization, diagnostic tests (X-rays, blood work, MRI), prescription medications, cancer treatment, and specialist care. Comprehensive plans may also cover wellness exams, vaccinations, dental cleanings, and hereditary conditions. Pre-existing conditions are typically NOT covered.

The best time to enroll is as early as possible — ideally when your pet is a puppy or kitten (8 weeks+). Premiums are lowest for young animals, and enrolling early means no pre-existing conditions. Once a condition is documented, it will likely be excluded from coverage permanently. Don't wait for the first health issue to appear.
