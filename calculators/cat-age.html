Frequently Asked Questions

Indoor cats typically live 12-18 years, while outdoor cats average only 2-5 years due to risks like traffic, predators, disease, and accidents. Our calculator provides a baseline — indoor cats may live longer and healthier lives with proper care.

Veterinarians typically classify cats as "senior" at 10-11 years and "geriatric" at 15+. However, many cats remain active and healthy well into their senior years. The AAFP (American Association of Feline Practitioners) recommends bi-annual checkups starting at age 10 to catch age-related conditions early.

Common age-related conditions in cats include: chronic kidney disease (affects 30-50% of cats over 15), hyperthyroidism, dental disease, arthritis, and cognitive dysfunction. Annual blood panels become essential for senior cats to catch these conditions in treatable stages.
