Frequently Asked Questions

Monthly dog food costs vary widely depending on your dog's size, activity level, and the brand you choose. For a typical 50-pound dog, budget dry food may cost $15 to $23 per month, mid-range options $28 to $42, premium kibble $47 to $67, and wet or raw diets can range from $100 to $240 per month. Large breeds or high-energy working dogs may require significantly more food, pushing costs toward the higher end of these ranges.

Premium dog foods are often more calorie-dense and nutrient-rich, meaning you may feed smaller portions while still meeting your dog's nutritional needs. Instead of comparing cost per bag, look at cost per calorie or cost per 1,000 kcal — this reveals the true value. A cheaper bag that requires larger servings can end up costing more over time. High-quality foods may also contribute to better long-term health, potentially reducing veterinary bills.

Buy larger bags or cases to lower the cost per pound by 20 to 30 percent. Sign up for subscription auto-delivery services, which often offer 5 to 15 percent discounts. Store dry food properly in a sealed container in a cool, dark place to prevent spoilage. Compare foods using cost per calorie rather than cost per bag, and consider mixing wet and dry food to provide the nutritional benefits of wet food at a lower overall cost.

💡 Money-Saving Tips

  • Buy larger bags — cost per lb drops 20-30%
  • Subscribe & save: 5-15% off auto-delivery
  • Store dry food properly (sealed, cool, dark)
  • Mix wet + dry: nutrition of wet, cost of dry
  • Check cost per 1,000 kcal, not cost per lb
